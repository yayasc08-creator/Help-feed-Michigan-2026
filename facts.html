Food Insecurity Facts

Key Statistics

  • 14.2% of Michigan residents face food insecurity
  • 1.5 million people affected statewide
  • 402,000+ children do not have consistent access to food
  • 13.7% of U.S. households experienced food insecurity in 2024

Who is Most Affected?

Food insecurity does not affect everyone equally. The most impacted groups include:

  • Low-income families
  • Children and students
  • Single-parent households
  • People with disabilities
  • Communities of color

Causes of Food Insecurity

  • Poverty and low wages
  • Unemployment or unstable jobs
  • High cost of living and housing
  • Lack of transportation
  • Limited access to grocery stores (food deserts)

Effects on Health

Food insecurity can lead to serious health issues including:

  • Diabetes and heart disease
  • Weakened immune system
  • Poor mental health
  • Delayed development in children
