Sources & References

This project uses trusted data from government agencies, nonprofit organizations, and national research groups focused on food insecurity and hunger relief.

📍 Michigan-Based Sources

  • Michigan Department of Health & Human Services (MDHHS)
    https://www.michigan.gov/mdhhs
    Used for SNAP, WIC, and assistance program information.
  • MI Bridges (Michigan Benefits Portal)
    https://newmibridges.michigan.gov/
    Official site to apply for food assistance programs.
  • Gleaners Community Food Bank
    https://www.gcfb.org
    Food distribution programs and data in Southeast Michigan.
  • Feeding America West Michigan
    https://www.feedwm.org
    Regional food bank serving West Michigan.
  • Food Bank of Eastern Michigan
    https://www.fbem.org
    Food distribution and hunger statistics in Eastern Michigan.
  • Greater Lansing Food Bank
    https://greaterlansingfoodbank.org
    Local food assistance programs and outreach.
  • Forgotten Harvest
    https://www.forgottenharvest.org
    Food rescue and distribution services in Metro Detroit.

🇺🇸 United States Sources

  • Feeding America
    https://www.feedingamerica.org
    National hunger statistics and food bank locator.
  • USDA (United States Department of Agriculture)
    https://www.usda.gov
    Information on SNAP, WIC, and federal food programs.
  • Hunger & Poverty Research (USDA ERS)
    https://www.ers.usda.gov
    Food insecurity data and national statistics.
  • United States Census Bureau
    https://www.census.gov
    Population and demographic data related to food insecurity.

📊 Data Usage

All data presented in this project is based on publicly available information from trusted organizations. This project is intended for educational and community awareness purposes.
